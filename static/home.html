Programme Overview
About ERITAS MDA

ERITAS stands for Evidence through Real-time Intelligence, Tracking, and Accountability Systems — the principle behind this platform: turning field data into real-time, accountable evidence that programme managers can act on.

Mass administration of azithromycin is a state-led preventive health campaign targeting children aged 1–59 months across every Local Government Area in the participating states. ERITAS hosts each state's campaign rounds side-by-side, in real time.

The campaign delivers Mass Drug Administration (MDA), a WHO-endorsed strategy for the control of Neglected Tropical Diseases (NTDs) and reduction of childhood mortality through the annual distribution of anthelmintics and azithromycin at the community level.

Field teams conduct household visits and capture treatment data via the Comcare mobile tool. This platform aggregates, validates and visualises that data in real time, supporting programme managers to track coverage against the WHO threshold of ≥ 80%.

Medicines Administered
Azithromycin (Antibiotic)
Targets trachoma and childhood pneumonia mortality as part of the WHO SAFE strategy. Weight-based dosing administered annually to eligible children aged 1–59 months. Distributed at the household level by trained community distributors.

Campaign Objectives
≥ 80% Administrative Coverage
WHO benchmark across all 23 LGAs, verified against pre-campaign baseline data.
NTD Elimination (SAFE Strategy)
Contributing to Nigeria's trachoma elimination roadmap through sustained MDA rounds.
Zero Missed Settlements
Geo-validated settlement tracking to ensure every community receives treatment.
Real-time Data Quality Assurance
Automated QC flags enable programme managers to correct field issues during the campaign window.
